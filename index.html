Year summary

Self-employment tax is a flat 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on 92.35% of cumulative net profit, with no wage-base cap — so it slightly overpays past the Social Security maximum. Half of the SE tax is deducted before applying your federal income-tax rate. 401(k) contributions reduce income tax only, not SE tax. State tax uses the same taxable base and does not add SE tax. This is a simplified estimator — it does not model tax brackets, deductions, the Social Security cap, or additional Medicare tax. Consult a tax professional for filing decisions.
